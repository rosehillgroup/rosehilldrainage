Build Your Drainage

Rosehill Interlocking Drainage Blocks are modular, high-void drainage units designed to be stacked and built into three-dimensional drainage structures.

A New Approach to Drainage

Unlike traditional drainage stackers — which are primarily laid in horizontal arrays — these blocks are purpose-designed for vertical construction. They interlock mechanically to form corners, steps, returns, and continuous drainage matrices.

The system allows engineers to build drainage into constrained or complex geometries while maintaining consistent void space and predictable hydraulic performance.

Engineered drainage that behaves like a structural building element.

What It Enables

  • Vertical stacking and interlocking
  • 90° corners, steps, and returns
  • Continuous open drainage matrices
  • Structural stability without loose aggregate
  • Hybrid builds with existing RDS stackers
  • Configurable height, depth, and geometry

Why Drainage Blocks?

There is a clear gap in current drainage options for constructed, three-dimensional drainage

Loose Aggregate

Traditional Method
  • Difficult to control geometry
  • Requires containment
  • Risk of migration or settlement
  • Labour-intensive placement
  • Poor repeatability

Plastic Crate Systems

Alternative
  • Fragile under point loads
  • Unsuitable for exposed applications
  • Limited stepped or cornered builds
  • Brittle in cold conditions
  • Risk of catastrophic cracking

Rosehill Drainage Block

Purpose-Designed
  • Mechanical interlocking in all directions
  • Vertical stacking with stability
  • Configurable corners, steps, returns
  • Impact-resistant recycled rubber
  • No aggregate, no containment needed

Buildable Drainage

Drainage becomes something you construct, not fill. Engineers can design in height, depth, and geometry for complex applications.

Structural Stability

Blocks interlock to create stable freestanding or retained builds without requiring concrete encasement. Reduces installation time and wet trades.

Controlled Void Matrix

Unlike loose aggregate: no settlement, no void collapse, no migration. Hydraulic performance is consistent and repeatable across the structure.

Faster Installation

No shovelling, levelling, or compaction stages. Installation becomes modular assembly — reducing labour and programme time on site.

Compact Footprint

Ideal where space is restricted, depth is limited, drainage must sit behind structural elements, or linear trenches must also carry load.

RDS Integration

Connects to Trough & Stacker systems and existing RDS products. Extends Rosehill Drainage from layered drainage into constructed drainage.

Rubber vs Plastic

Unlike plastic crate systems, recycled rubber blocks offer inherent material advantages for demanding drainage applications

Impact Resistance

Rubber absorbs impact energy without fracturing, maintaining structural integrity under point loads and ground movement

Ground Movement Flexibility

Natural flexibility accommodates settlement and thermal movement without cracking or losing interlock

Cold-Weather Performance

No brittleness in freezing conditions. Rubber maintains its mechanical properties across extreme temperature ranges

No Catastrophic Failure

Unlike plastic which cracks and collapses, rubber deforms elastically and returns to shape — no sudden loss of drainage capacity
